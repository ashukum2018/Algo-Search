Given an integer array nums, return the number of AND triples. An AND triple is a triple of indices (i, j, k) such that:</h4> 0 <= i < nums.length 0 <= j < nums.length 0 <= k < nums.length nums[i] & nums[j] & nums[k] == 0, where & represents the bitwise-AND operator. <br><h4>Example 1:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> nums = [2,1,3] <br><h4>Output:</h4> 12 <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> We could choose the following i, j, k triples:</h4> (i=0, j=0, k=1) :</h4> 2 & 2 & 1 (i=0, j=1, k=0) :</h4> 2 & 1 & 2 (i=0, j=1, k=1) :</h4> 2 & 1 & 1 (i=0, j=1, k=2) :</h4> 2 & 1 & 3 (i=0, j=2, k=1) :</h4> 2 & 3 & 1 (i=1, j=0, k=0) :</h4> 1 & 2 & 2 (i=1, j=0, k=1) :</h4> 1 & 2 & 1 (i=1, j=0, k=2) :</h4> 1 & 2 & 3 (i=1, j=1, k=0) :</h4> 1 & 1 & 2 (i=1, j=2, k=0) :</h4> 1 & 3 & 2 (i=2, j=0, k=1) :</h4> 3 & 2 & 1 (i=2, j=1, k=0) :</h4> 3 & 1 & 2 <br><h4>Example 2:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> nums = [0,0,0] <br><h4>Output:</h4> 27 <br><h4>Constraints:</h4> 1 <= nums.length <= 1000 0 <= nums[i] < 216
