Given an integer num, find the closest two integers in absolute difference whose product equals num + 1 or num + 2. Return the two integers in any order. <br><h4>Example 1:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> num = 8 <br><h4>Output:</h4> [3,3] <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> For num + 1 = 9, the closest divisors are 3 & 3, for num + 2 = 10, the closest divisors are 2 & 5, hence 3 & 3 is chosen. <br><h4>Example 2:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> num = 123 <br><h4>Output:</h4> [5,25] <br><h4>Example 3:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> num = 999 <br><h4>Output:</h4> [40,25] <br><h4>Constraints:</h4> 1 <= num <= 10^9
