Given a rectangle of size n x m, return the minimum number of integer-sided squares that tile the rectangle. <br><h4>Example 1:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 2, m = 3 <br><h4>Output:</h4> 3 <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> 3 squares are necessary to cover the rectangle. 2 (squares of 1x1) 1 (square of 2x2) <br><h4>Example 2:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 5, m = 8 <br><h4>Output:</h4> 5 <br><h4>Example 3:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 11, m = 13 <br><h4>Output:</h4> 6 <br><h4>Constraints:</h4> 1 <= n, m <= 13
