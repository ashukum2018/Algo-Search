Given an m x n integer matrix heightMap representing the height of each unit cell in a 2D elevation map, return the volume of water it can trap after raining. <br><h4>Example 1:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> heightMap = [[1,4,3,1,3,2],[3,2,1,3,2,4],[2,3,3,2,3,1]] <br><h4>Output:</h4> 4 <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> After the rain, water is trapped between the blocks. We have two small ponds 1 and 3 units trapped. The total volume of water trapped is 4. <br><h4>Example 2:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> heightMap = [[3,3,3,3,3],[3,2,2,2,3],[3,2,1,2,3],[3,2,2,2,3],[3,3,3,3,3]] <br><h4>Output:</h4> 10 <br><h4>Constraints:</h4> m == heightMap.length n == heightMap[i].length 1 <= m, n <= 200 0 <= heightMap[i][j] <= 2 * 104
