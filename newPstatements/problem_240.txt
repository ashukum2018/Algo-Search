An ugly number is a positive integer whose prime factors are limited to 2, 3, and 5. Given an integer n, return true if n is an ugly number. <br><h4>Example 1:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 6 <br><h4>Output:</h4> true <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> 6 = 2 \xc3\x97 3 <br><h4>Example 2:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 1 <br><h4>Output:</h4> true <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> 1 has no prime factors, therefore all of its prime factors are limited to 2, 3, and 5. <br><h4>Example 3:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 14 <br><h4>Output:</h4> false <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> 14 is not ugly since it includes the prime factor 7. <br><h4>Constraints:</h4> -231 <= n <= 231 - 1
