Given a positive integer, check whether it has alternating bits:</h4> namely, if two adjacent bits will always have different values. <br><h4>Example 1:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 5 <br><h4>Output:</h4> true <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> The binary representation of 5 is:</h4> 101 <br><h4>Example 2:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 7 <br><h4>Output:</h4> false <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> The binary representation of 7 is:</h4> 111. <br><h4>Example 3:</h4> <br><h4>Input:</h4> n = 11 <br><h4>Output:</h4> false <br><h4>Explanation:</h4> The binary representation of 11 is:</h4> 1011. <br><h4>Constraints:</h4> 1 <= n <= 231 - 1
